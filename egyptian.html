About the Civilization

The Egyptian Civilization, centered along the Nile River, developed around 3100 BCE and lasted for over three millennia. Ancient Egypt is renowned for its monumental architecture, especially the iconic pyramids, temples, and statues. Egyptians had a polytheistic belief system, with gods and goddesses influencing every aspect of life and death. They believed in the afterlife, and this is reflected in their elaborate tombs and mummification practices. The Nile River was central to Egyptian life, providing water for agriculture and transportation. Egyptian society contributed significantly to art, writing (hieroglyphs), science, and governance, shaping much of the Mediterranean and Near Eastern world. Their achievements in art and architecture remain a source of wonder today.
