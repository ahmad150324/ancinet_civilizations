About the Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization, also known as the Harappan Civilization, thrived around 2500 BCE in the northwestern regions of South Asia, primarily in present-day Pakistan and northwest India. It is one of the world's earliest urban cultures, known for its advanced city planning, architecture, and social organization. The cities of Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa featured well-organized streets, drainage systems, and large public baths, indicating a high level of civic planning.
