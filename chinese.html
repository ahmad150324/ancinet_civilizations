About the Civilization

Ancient Chinese Civilization developed along the Yellow River (Huang He) and Yangtze River valleys around 1600 BCE. It is one of the oldest continuous civilizations in the world, contributing significantly to philosophy, literature, art, and science. Early dynasties like the Shang and Zhou laid the foundations of Chinese culture, introducing writing, bronze working, and centralized governance. The Chinese developed complex philosophies, including Confucianism and Daoism, which influenced their way of life and governance. Ancient China is also known for its remarkable inventions, such as paper, gunpowder, and the compass.
