About the Civilization

The Mesopotamian Civilization, often called the "Cradle of Civilization," flourished between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers in modern-day Iraq. Emerging around 3500 BCE, Mesopotamia included notable cultures like the Sumerians, Akkadians, Babylonians, and Assyrians. It is known for inventing the earliest writing system, cuneiform, and making advances in mathematics, astronomy, and law. The famous Code of Hammurabi, one of the oldest written law codes, was created here. Mesopotamians built impressive ziggurats (temple towers) and engaged in complex trade networks.
